

HTML entities are special characters or reserved symbols in HTML that are represented by a specific code to avoid conflicts in rendering. 
These entities begin with an ampersand (&) and end with a semicolon (;). 
They are commonly used to display characters that have special meaning in HTML or are not easily typed on a keyboard.



 five commonly used HTML entities: 
1.&amp; Ampersand
2.&quot; Double quotation mark
3.&cent; Cent
4.&gt; Greater than
5.&apos; Single quotation mark
